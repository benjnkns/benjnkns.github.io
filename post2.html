The Art of Saying "No" (Or, How I Learned to Say "Yes" Anyway)

by Ben

Posted on January 10, 2024 at 9:00 AM

They say mastering the art of saying "no" is one of the most powerful tools for achieving your goals. But what happens when "no" isn’t an option? What if, like me, you live in a karmic system where turning down a friend’s request feels like exchanging your net worth for bad vibes and cosmic IOUs?

Spoiler alert: I’m terrible at saying no. My friends know it, the universe knows it, and honestly, I think even my cat knows it. But instead of fighting this uphill battle, I’ve found a way to work with it. Enter: Robert Kiyosaki’s approach to saying "yes" and figuring out how to make it work.

The Struggle: Balancing My Goals with Saying "Yes"

Let me set the scene: I’m on a 12-week mission. It’s focused, intense, and packed with tasks that require my time, energy, and razor-sharp discipline. And yet, life doesn’t seem to care about my neatly laid plans. Friends invite me out, family asks for help, and opportunities pop up that don’t exactly align with my goals.

The old advice would have me calmly and firmly say, “No, thank you. I’m working on something important.” But in my world, turning down these requests feels like dropping the cosmic ball. Karma’s exchange rate is brutal—say no to a friend, and suddenly, my mission feels less supported. Not ideal.

The Pivot: Kiyosaki’s Method of Saying "Yes"

So, instead of trying to master "no," I’m pivoting to a Kiyosaki-inspired mindset: always saying “yes,” but asking myself a better question—“How can I make this work?” This isn’t about blindly agreeing to everything and drowning in commitments. It’s about creative problem-solving.

For example, if a friend asks me to hang out, instead of canceling my work session, I find a compromise. Maybe we hang out while I do something productive, or I reschedule the hangout to fit better into my week. If an unexpected expense pops up, I don’t shut down my budget. Instead, I look for ways to create extra income or reprioritize my spending.

Saying "yes" doesn’t mean abandoning my mission—it means building flexibility into my plan. Sure, the exchange rate isn’t perfect. Sometimes it feels like I’m giving more than I’m getting back in the short term. But in the grand scheme, saying “yes” keeps me aligned with my values and, surprisingly, helps me move forward.

The Takeaway: "Yes" with Boundaries

Here’s the kicker: saying "yes" isn’t about overextending yourself. It’s about aligning your actions with your goals while still honoring the people and opportunities around you. Think of it as financial judo—using the momentum of life’s requests to propel yourself toward your mission.

Is it perfect? Nope. Does it work? Surprisingly, yes. By leaning into the "yes," I’ve found ways to grow, strengthen relationships, and stay true to my goals without burning bridges—or karma points.

So, How Will You Say "Yes" Today?

If you’re like me, stuck in the delicate balance of pursuing a mission while navigating life’s requests, try this: the next time you want to say "no," pause. Ask yourself, “How can I say ‘yes’ and make this work for me?” You might be surprised at the doors it opens.

Because sometimes, the best way forward isn’t shutting the door—it’s finding a way to keep it open and walk through with confidence.
